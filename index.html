Era 1 — Creature Selector

Choose the lineage of your dungeon's inhabitants. Each evolutionary path leads to a unique D&D race that will define your playstyle, bonuses, and flavour for the rest of the run. The evolution tree is not yet implemented — the list below is the current candidate roster for review and expansion.

Draconic & Reptilian

Draconic Chromatic Dragon Apex predator of the skies — scales, talons, elemental breath, and insatiable greed.
Draconic Metallic Dragon Noble and ancient; gold, silver, or bronze — wisdom tempered by draconic pride.
Draconic Lizardfolk Cold-blooded and pragmatic; survival above all else, no concept of waste.
Draconic Kobold Small, cunning, and numerous — masters of traps and tunnel warfare.
Draconic Yuan-ti Serpentine flesh and cold intellect; they traded their humanity for power.

Undead

Undead Skeleton Mindless bone given purpose; tireless, fearless, and indifferent to pain.
Undead Zombie Shambling brute — slow but relentless, overwhelms through sheer numbers.
Undead Vampire Aristocratic predator of the night; charm, blood, and centuries of scheming.
Undead Lich An archmage who cheated death; trades mortality for phylactery-bound immortality.
Undead Wight A fallen warrior cursed with undeath; retains battle cunning, loses all mercy.
Undead Ghoul Ravenous flesh-hungry predator; paralysing claws and an endless appetite.

Goblinoids & Beastkin

Goblinoid Goblin Chaotic, opportunistic, and infuriatingly clever — strength in numbers.
Goblinoid Hobgoblin Disciplined and militaristic; the goblinoid race that chose order over chaos.
Goblinoid Bugbear Brutish ambush predator; enormous, stealthy, and startlingly violent.
Goblinoid Orc Fierce tribal warrior; raw strength, relentless aggression, savage honour.
Goblinoid Gnoll Hyena-headed marauders blessed (or cursed) by the demon lord Yeenoghu.

Aberrations

Aberration Mind Flayer Illithid psionicists from the Far Realm; enslave lesser minds, harvest intellect.
Aberration Beholder Floating eye tyrant; paranoid, megalomaniacal, and bristling with antimagic.
Aberration Aboleth Ancient aquatic elder — remembers the world before gods, and resents them for it.
Aberration Gibbering Mouther A chaotic mass of eyes and mouths; its babble drives listeners to madness.
Aberration Nothic A wizard rotted into a one-eyed wretch by dark knowledge; reads minds, hoards secrets.

Oozes & Elementals

Ooze Gelatinous Cube A perfectly cuboid acidic horror; silent, patient, and invisible until it's too late.
Ooze Black Pudding Corrosive amorphous ooze; splitting it only makes more problems.
Elemental Fire Elemental Living flame given will; consumes everything, illuminates nothing of worth.
Elemental Earth Elemental Stone and soil incarnate — slow to move, impossible to stop.
Elemental Shadow Demon Void given form; thrives in darkness, withers in light, devours hope.

Monstrous

Monstrous Harpy Winged predators with an irresistible song; they lure sailors to their doom.
Monstrous Medusa Snake-haired petrifier; her gaze turns the brave to garden ornaments.
Monstrous Minotaur Bull-headed labyrinth guardian; strength, rage, and an unerring sense of direction.
Monstrous Troll Regenerating swamp giant; only fire and acid can truly end one.
Monstrous Werewolf Lycanthrope torn between beast and man; spreads its curse with every bite.
Monstrous Naga Serpentine spellcaster; ancient, narcissistic, and nearly impossible to permanently kill.

Fiends

Fiend Imp Tiny devil familiar; insignificant alone, terrifying as a colony.
Fiend Cambion Half-demon hybrid; all the charm of their fiendish parent, twice the ambition.
Fiend Barbed Devil Thorned infernal enforcer; methodical, cruel, and loyal only to hierarchy.
Fiend Night Hag Dreamstealing witch who traffics in souls; older than most gods, wiser than most.

Humanoid Offshoots

Humanoid Kenku Raven-folk cursed with voicelessness; communicate through flawless mimicry.
Humanoid Tabaxi Agile cat-folk driven by insatiable curiosity; fast, graceful, easily distracted.
Humanoid Aarakocra Eagle-winged bird-folk from mountaintop eyries; natural scouts and messengers.
Humanoid Tortle Shell-bound wanderers; slow, deeply wise, and extraordinarily hard to kill.
Humanoid Centaur Half-human, half-horse; fierce tribal warriors of the open plains.

About

Dungeon Keeper v0.16 — You are the keeper of a fledgling dungeon. Gather resources, house creatures, and expand your underground empire. Keep your creatures fed or watch them desert you — or worse.

Build a Lair to unlock housing, stockpile food to attract creatures, then assign them to buildings. Each Farm worker produces 2 food/s but every creature eats 1 food/s. Expand carefully — starvation kills.
