The Age Begins
I

Before there was hunger, before there was purpose — there was only the dark. You did not arrive. You simply were. A will without a body, a mind pressed between the stones of a world that had forgotten you were there.

II

Then — sensation. The weight of stone above. The slow drip of water somewhere far below. Something had changed in the world above, and the tremor of it reached you, faint as a whispered rumour. You were not summoned. But something had drawn near.

III

These halls are yours by right of ruin. No deed records them. No king claims them. They were abandoned to silence, and silence answered to no one — until now. A dungeon without a mind is merely a grave. You are the difference.

IV

You reach — not with hands, but with intent. The pick stirs. The stones remember what they once were: not a tomb, but a stronghold. Not an ending, but a beginning. Your first choice awaits. Make it count, Dungeon Mind. The age has only just begun.

Contract of Cession & Continuance

Executed between THE UNDERSIGNED KEEPER and the Amnizu ZAGRIVETH, Clerk-Tyrant of the Fifth Ledger, hereafter "the Bank."

§1. The Keeper cedes to the Bank the whole of the demesne: every stone laid, every larder filled, every soul housed therein. The Bank's claim is total, immediate, and without appeal.

§2. In consideration, the Bank shall remit to the Keeper such Quintessence as the demesne has distilled — the only currency that survives the ending of things.

§3. The Keeper shall wake again upon unfamiliar ground, remembering everything and owning nothing. The Bank considers this a kindness. The Bank is not required to explain why.

DungeonKeepers

Deep beneath the Material Plane, where light has never reached, something stirs. Not a creature or a god, but older — a pocket of raw arcane will seeping into existance from the Weave of magic itself. A Dungeon Mind, newly conscious and hungry to become something more. At the heart of every proper Dungeon across the planes is a single Dungeon Mind, a sentient being.

You are that mind. You have no body, no voice, no name — only stone, will, and the faint pulse of eldritch magic humming from the Underdark below. Attract creatures that resonate with your nature: goblins, dragons, undead, fey, or something stranger still. They will build your chambers, research the arcane arts, and protect you. By exerting your influence, you can shape the world around you, but only if you are clever and patient enough to do so.

It is time for you to Awaken and begin your journey. Grow strong enough, and you will build mighty stone halls and challenge other Dungeons across the planes. Evolve, conquer, or fall into obscurity — forgotten by the ages like so many dungeons before you.
