Dungeon Keeper

You are a will without a body — a dungeon consciousness newly awakened in the Material Plane. No hands to build. No voice to command. Not yet able to manifest a physical form. To reclaim your power and forge a physical form, you need help.

Drawn by an instinct to your presence, feeling a synergy to your projected affinity, a group of creatures has gathered nearby. They don't know what you are — only that something called, and they answered. Grow your power, guide them, and build through them. Once they are settled into a thriving community, they may be able to help you. They can eventually give you the body you need to truly rule.

Dungeon Keeper is an incremental dungeon-management game based on the classic Dungeons & Dragons setting. Every run is different, so you'll need to adapt your strategy each time. Settle a new race, gather resources, grow your population, raise buildings, research upgrades, and prestige to carry bonuses into future runs and unlock new content. You won't see all content each run, but each time you prestige you will carry permanent bonuses into future runs. These should help you progress further in each new run, seeing more content and achieving greater success. Achievements will be unlocked as you progress, providing additional rewards, races, and other bonuses.

Gameplay

Time moves continuously, tracked as Day, Year, and Season in the header. Everything in your dungeon — resource production, population growth, research — ticks forward as time passes. Offline time is saved, and is automatically used when you return.

The Game Phases

Each run is divided into multiple phases. The first is the awakening phase: you have no body, only will. You have only just manifested, and are still growing in power and control of your surroundings. You need to attract a group of creatures, drawn to your presence, to serve as your hands. You guide them through the Awakening tab — a skill tree of actions that grow your early control and influence, and ultimately unlock the passage to the next phase.

Phase 2 begins when a small group of a selected race of creatures accept you and your guidance. You have no domain of your own yet, so you shall just have to borrow theirs. They are a small band now, but they are yours. If you can protect them and build a strong foundation, you can expand your domain and eventually create a Dungeon Core to create a real body for yourself.

Resources

Resources fall into three tiers of increasing complexity. Tier 1 materials are gathered manually or produced by simple buildings. Tier 2 requires converter buildings that consume Tier 1 inputs. Tier 3 materials are rare magical substances at the end of long processing chains. Storage buildings can raise material caps as needed.

Coins

Coins are a parallel currency tracked in the classic Dungeons and Dragons style, measured in copper pieces (cp), displayed as gold, silver, and copper (100 cp = 1 sp, 10 sp = 1 gp), capped at 1,000 coins to start. Storage buildings have no effect on coins, but other buildings might. Passive coin income requires the Taxation research, after which your dungeon earns coins per day based on your current population, their jobs, and other factors. Many mid-to-late buildings cost coins in addition to materials.

Population & Jobs

Your creature count grows toward a cap set by your housing. Hovels (or other thematically named buildings) and other unlockable housings each contribute to that cap. Population in general needs food — a sustained deficit causes starvation which can kill your population. Creatures often need to fill job slots at production buildings in order for them to function; the Population panel shows employed creatures out of total available slots. All population starts as peseants for their defualt job.

Build Tab

The Build tab holds manual gather actions — Scavenge Food, Fell a Tree, and Break Stones — alongside all constructable buildings. Hovels add housing. Storage raises every material cap. Production buildings create job slots that turn idle creatures into steady resource income. New building types unlock automatically once prerequisite counts are met.

Research Tab

The Research tab unlocks technologies that improve your race's output, population ceiling, and late-game capabilities. Technologies are organized by tier; earlier unlocks gate access to more powerful options deeper in the tree.

Biome & Race

Every run begins with a choice of what race to play as: these are the creatures that will populate your dungeon. Not every race is equally suited to every biome. The biome is where you manifested in the Material Plane (which sets starting resources and persistent modifiers) and is randomly selected at game start. Later runs will eventually allow a method (prestige option) to choose your own biome! The combination of biome and race shapes production efficiency, growth rates, and the flavour of your run. See the Biomes and Creatures pages for the full reference.

Prestige

When a run ends you can prestige — resetting your dungeon in exchange for lasting bonuses that persist into future runs. Prestige count and currencyis tracked globally across all runs.

Biomes

A biome is the terrain you manifested in, and is set at the start of each run. Every biome grants a package of starting resources and shapes how well each creature type performs for the rest of the run. Try to match your chosen race with a biome that suits it best! Modifiers are randomly assigned at the time of biome selection and vary each run. The 25 biomes are listed below, grouped into Natural, D&D Lore, and Magical origins.

Creature Suitability Scale

Every creature has a preferred biome (▲) and a difficult biome (▼). A creature's suitability for the active biome determines the production and population-growth bonuses or penalties it receives, on the scale below.

    Idle Save Manager — Companion Extension

    Idle Save Manager is an optional Chrome extension built specifically for Dungeon Keepers. It automatically syncs your save to your Google account so you can pick up your run on any computer without ever touching an export string.

    The extension popup — one click to push your save to the cloud.

    Why use it?

    Dungeon Keepers is an offline-progress game — your run accumulates over real-world time. Without the extension, swapping between machines means manually exporting your save, transferring the string, and importing it on the other side. Miss that step and you lose hours of banked progress. The extension handles all of that for you silently in the background.

    ◆ Auto-Sync

    Whenever you make meaningful progress, the extension pushes your save to chrome.storage.sync — Google's built-in cloud storage for extensions. On your other machine, open the game and the latest save is pulled down automatically.

    ◆ Zero Account Required

    The extension piggybacks on your existing Chrome sign-in. No new accounts, no external servers, no subscription — just install and forget about it.

    ◆ Conflict Detection

    If the cloud save is newer than your local save when you open the game, the extension alerts you and lets you choose which one to keep — so you never silently overwrite a longer run.

    Installation

    The extension is free and open-source. Because it is not yet published to the Chrome Web Store, installation is a quick one-time manual step:

    1. Visit the Idle Save Manager GitHub page and download or clone the repository.
    2. Open Chrome and navigate to chrome://extensions.
    3. Enable Developer mode using the toggle in the top-right corner.
    4. Click Load unpacked and select the folder you downloaded.
    5. The extension icon will appear in your toolbar. Sign into Chrome if you haven't already, and you're done.

    How it works day-to-day

    Once installed there is nothing to configure. The extension watches for save activity in the background and syncs automatically. On machines where you want the save pulled down, simply open the game — the extension fetches the latest cloud save on page load and applies it if it's newer than what's stored locally.

    The extension popup (click the toolbar icon) shows when your save was last synced and lets you manually push or pull if needed — useful if you want to force a sync before shutting down a machine.

    Privacy

    Your save data is stored exclusively in chrome.storage.sync, which is encrypted in transit and tied to your Google account. It is never sent to any third-party server. The extension requests only the storage permission and access to the Dungeon Keepers tab.
